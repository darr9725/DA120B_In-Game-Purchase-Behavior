# 4. Problem Statement and Introduction

### Date: 03 May 2025

The team collaborated on the first formal deliverable, consisting of:

### Problem Statement Summary

The team identified a gap in understanding what motivates mobile gamers to make in-game purchases.

Several variables were identified as potentially influential:

* Age
* Gender
* Geographic location
* Session behavior
* Game genre preferences
* Engagement levels
* Spending history

The objective of the project became understanding how these factors influence purchasing decisions and identifying patterns that may support business decision-making within the gaming industry.

# 6. Dataset Evaluation and Selection

### Date: 16 May 2025

The team evaluated multiple Kaggle datasets.

### Datasets Considered

Features included:

* Age

* Purchase Amount

#### Mobile Game User Data Analysis Dataset

A behavioral dataset focused on user activity and purchasing patterns.

#### Predict Online Gaming Behavior Dataset

A larger behavioral dataset containing approximately 40,000 observations.

### Evaluation Criteria

The team compared datasets based on:

* Dataset size
* Data quality
* Missing values
* Analytical usefulness
* Business relevance
* Visualization potential

### Final Dataset Selection

The team selected:

**Predict Online Gaming Behavior Dataset**

### Reasons for Selection

* Largest dataset available.
* Approximately 40,000 records.
* Clean structure.
* Strong analytical potential.
* Suitable for statistical analysis and predictive insights.

Several gacha-related and review-based datasets were considered as optional references but were not selected as primary analytical sources.

### Review of Deliverables

Following a detailed review, the team determined that the submitted workbook primarily consisted of prepared datasets and summary sheets rather than a complete visualization solution.

While the material contained useful supporting information, much of the work overlapped with outputs already generated during the data preparation phase.

More importantly, the project still lacked:

* Interactive dashboards
* Analytical visualizations

* Executive-level reporting views

# Lessons Learned

Throughout the project, the team identified several important lessons:

* Dataset quality is more important than dataset availability.
* Clear ownership of responsibilities is critical for project execution.
* Documentation should be maintained continuously rather than at the end of the project.
* Teams and GitHub alignment improves collaboration and version control.
* Data cleaning and preparation significantly influence the quality of subsequent analysis.
* Periodic progress reviews are necessary to identify gaps and reallocate responsibilities when required.

The project evolved from a broad gaming analytics idea into a structured analysis of mobile gaming purchase behavior supported by data engineering, exploratory analysis, business intelligence, documentation, and project management practices.
