# **In-Game-Purchase - Behavior Analysis**

## Brief Introduction

The mobile gaming industry has become one of the fastest-growing sectors in the global digital entertainment industry and is estimated to reach $256.19 billion by 2030. The overall goal of this research is to identify and understand the behavioral and demographic characteristics that impact mobile gamers' decisions to make in-game purchases.

## Problem Statement

Despite the fast growth of mobile games, little has been studied about what drives consumers to buy content in-game. Several important characteristics of players such as demographics, session behavior, game genre preferences, and amount of time played may influence whether someone buys in a game, but there is not wide knowledge and understanding about the relationship between these factors. Therefore, developers and other stakeholders cannot determine which types of players are easier to attract into in-game purchases and what types of behaviors or actions result in purchasing decisions.

## Business Questions

1. Which player demographics are most likely to spend money?
2. How does player engagement influence purchasing behavior?
3. Which game genres generate the highest revenue?
4. What spending patterns exist among different player segments?
5. How quickly do players convert into paying customers?
6. Which payment methods are associated with the highest spending?
7. Are there significant differences in spending behavior across countries?
8. Does longer play time lead to higher purchase amounts?
9. Which player profile represents the highest-value customer?
10. What factors most strongly influence spending behavior?
